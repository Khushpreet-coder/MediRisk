TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
PURPOSE: Evaluates thyroid gland function. Diagnoses hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, thyroiditis, Graves' disease, and Hashimoto's disease.

---

PARAMETER: TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: mIU/L
Meaning: TSH is produced by the pituitary gland to stimulate the thyroid to produce T3 and T4. It is the primary and most sensitive screening marker for thyroid dysfunction.
Normal Range: 0.4–4.0 mIU/L
Low Interpretation: Low TSH indicates hyperthyroidism — the thyroid is overactive and producing excess hormones, suppressing TSH. Causes include Graves' disease, toxic nodules, or excess thyroid hormone intake.
High Interpretation: High TSH indicates hypothyroidism — the thyroid is underactive and not producing enough hormones, causing the pituitary to signal more. Causes include Hashimoto's disease, iodine deficiency, or thyroid surgery.

---

PARAMETER: T3 (Total Triiodothyronine)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: ng/dL
Meaning: T3 is the more active thyroid hormone. Total T3 measures both free and bound T3 in blood. It regulates metabolism, heart rate, and energy.
Normal Range: 80–200 ng/dL
Low Interpretation: Low T3 may indicate hypothyroidism, severe illness (euthyroid sick syndrome), or malnutrition.
High Interpretation: High T3 suggests hyperthyroidism or T3 toxicosis. Symptoms include weight loss, palpitations, and anxiety.

---

PARAMETER: Free T3 (Free Triiodothyronine)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: pg/mL
Meaning: Free T3 measures only the unbound, biologically active fraction of T3. More accurate than total T3 as it is unaffected by binding protein levels.
Normal Range: 2.3–4.2 pg/mL
Low Interpretation: Low free T3 indicates hypothyroidism or severe systemic illness.
High Interpretation: High free T3 confirms hyperthyroidism, especially in Graves' disease or T3 toxicosis.

---

PARAMETER: T4 (Total Thyroxine)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: µg/dL
Meaning: T4 is the main hormone secreted by the thyroid gland. It is converted to the more active T3 in peripheral tissues. Total T4 includes bound and unbound fractions.
Normal Range: 5.0–12.0 µg/dL
Low Interpretation: Low T4 indicates hypothyroidism. Common causes include Hashimoto's thyroiditis, iodine deficiency, or thyroid surgery.
High Interpretation: High T4 suggests hyperthyroidism or excess thyroid hormone intake.

---

PARAMETER: Free T4 (Free Thyroxine)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: ng/dL
Meaning: Free T4 measures unbound, active thyroxine. It is more accurate than total T4 and is preferred for diagnosing thyroid disorders.
Normal Range: 0.8–1.8 ng/dL
Low Interpretation: Low free T4 confirms hypothyroidism, especially when TSH is also elevated.
High Interpretation: High free T4 confirms hyperthyroidism, especially when TSH is suppressed.

---

PARAMETER: TPO Antibodies (Thyroid Peroxidase Antibodies)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: IU/mL
Meaning: TPO antibodies are produced when the immune system mistakenly attacks thyroid tissue. Their presence indicates autoimmune thyroid disease.
Normal Range: < 35 IU/mL
Low Interpretation: Negative — no autoimmune thyroid disease detected.
High Interpretation: Elevated TPO antibodies indicate autoimmune thyroid disease — most commonly Hashimoto's thyroiditis (hypothyroid) or Graves' disease (hyperthyroid).

---

PARAMETER: TGAb (Thyroglobulin Antibodies)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: IU/mL
Meaning: TGAb are antibodies against thyroglobulin, a protein made by the thyroid. Used alongside TPO antibodies to diagnose autoimmune thyroid disease.
Normal Range: < 20 IU/mL
Low Interpretation: Negative — no autoimmune thyroid disease detected.
High Interpretation: Elevated TGAb suggests Hashimoto's thyroiditis or Graves' disease. Also used to monitor thyroid cancer recurrence after thyroidectomy.

---

PARAMETER: TSI (Thyroid Stimulating Immunoglobulin)
TEST: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
Unit: Index
Meaning: TSI are antibodies that mimic TSH and stimulate the thyroid to overproduce hormones. Specific marker for Graves' disease.
Normal Range: < 1.3 index
Low Interpretation: Negative — Graves' disease not indicated.
High Interpretation: Elevated TSI confirms Graves' disease, an autoimmune cause of hyperthyroidism. Useful for distinguishing Graves' from other causes of hyperthyroidism.
