TEST: Blood Glucose Test
PURPOSE: Measures the amount of glucose (sugar) in the blood. Used to screen, diagnose, and monitor diabetes (Type 1, Type 2, gestational).

---

PARAMETER: Fasting Blood Glucose (FBG)
TEST: Blood Glucose Test
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Measures blood glucose level after 8–12 hours of fasting. Primary test for diagnosing diabetes.
Normal Range: 70–99 mg/dL
Prediabetes Range: 100–125 mg/dL
Diabetes Threshold: ≥ 126 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: < 70 mg/dL indicates hypoglycemia — may be caused by medications, fasting, liver disease, or hormonal disorders.
High Interpretation: 100–125 mg/dL suggests prediabetes. ≥ 126 mg/dL on two separate tests confirms diabetes. May indicate insulin resistance or pancreatic dysfunction.
Preparation: Patient must fast for 8–12 hours before test. Water is allowed.

---

PARAMETER: Random Blood Glucose (RBG)
TEST: Blood Glucose Test
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Blood glucose measured at any time regardless of last meal. Used for quick screening.
Normal Range: < 140 mg/dL
Diabetes Threshold: ≥ 200 mg/dL (with symptoms)
Low Interpretation: Values below 70 mg/dL suggest hypoglycemia.
High Interpretation: ≥ 200 mg/dL with symptoms (thirst, urination, fatigue) indicates possible diabetes.
Preparation: No fasting required.

---

PARAMETER: Postprandial Blood Glucose (PPBG)
TEST: Blood Glucose Test
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Blood glucose measured exactly 2 hours after eating. Evaluates how well the body processes glucose after a meal.
Normal Range: < 140 mg/dL
Prediabetes Range: 140–199 mg/dL
Diabetes Threshold: ≥ 200 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: Values below 70 mg/dL may indicate reactive hypoglycemia.
High Interpretation: 140–199 mg/dL suggests impaired glucose tolerance (prediabetes). ≥ 200 mg/dL indicates diabetes.
Preparation: Patient eats a standard meal and blood is drawn exactly 2 hours later.

---

PARAMETER: Capillary Glucose Level
TEST: Blood Glucose Test
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Glucose measured from a finger prick using a glucometer. Used for self-monitoring and quick bedside testing.
Normal Range (Fasting): 70–99 mg/dL
Normal Range (Post-meal): < 140 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: < 70 mg/dL indicates hypoglycemia.
High Interpretation: Elevated values may indicate poor glucose control or undiagnosed diabetes. Results slightly vary from venous plasma values.
Note: Less accurate than lab-based venous plasma tests. Confirm abnormal values with lab testing.

---

PARAMETER: Venous Plasma Glucose
TEST: Blood Glucose Test
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Gold standard glucose test using blood drawn from a vein and analyzed in a lab. More accurate than capillary testing.
Normal Range (Fasting): 70–99 mg/dL
Normal Range (Random): < 140 mg/dL
Normal Range (2-hour Post-meal): < 140 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: < 70 mg/dL indicates hypoglycemia.
High Interpretation: Elevated venous plasma glucose confirms diabetes or prediabetes depending on threshold exceeded.
Note: Used as confirmation test when capillary readings are abnormal.
