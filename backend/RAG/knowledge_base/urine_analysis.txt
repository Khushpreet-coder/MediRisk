Test: Urinalysis (Urine Analysis Test)

Purpose:
Urinalysis is a diagnostic test that evaluates the physical, chemical, and microscopic properties of urine. It is used to detect infections, kidney disorders, liver disease, diabetes, and urinary tract abnormalities.

------------------------------------------------------------

Parameters:

1. Physical (Visual Examination)
- Color
- Clarity (Clear / Cloudy / Turbid)

2. Chemical (Dipstick Test)
- pH (Acidity)
- Protein
- Glucose
- Ketones
- Bilirubin
- Blood (Hemoglobin)
- Nitrites
- Leukocyte Esterase
- Specific Gravity

3. Microscopic Examination
- Red Blood Cells (RBC)
- White Blood Cells (WBC)
- Epithelial Cells
- Bacteria
- Yeast / Parasites
- Crystals
- Urinary Casts

------------------------------------------------------------

Normal Findings (General):

Color: Pale yellow to amber  
Clarity: Clear  
pH: 4.5 – 8.0  
Protein: Negative / Trace  
Glucose: Negative  
Ketones: Negative  
Bilirubin: Negative  
Blood: Negative  
Nitrites: Negative  
Leukocyte Esterase: Negative  
Specific Gravity: 1.005 – 1.030  

RBC: 0–2 / HPF  
WBC: 0–5 / HPF  
Epithelial Cells: Few  
Bacteria: None  
Crystals: None or occasional  
Casts: None or few hyaline casts  

------------------------------------------------------------

Interpretation:

- Protein present ? kidney disease, hypertension, diabetes
- Glucose present ? diabetes mellitus
- Ketones present ? diabetic ketoacidosis, starvation
- Blood present ? infection, stones, trauma, cancer
- Nitrites positive ? bacterial urinary tract infection (UTI)
- Leukocyte esterase positive ? infection/inflammation
- High WBC ? infection or inflammation
- High RBC ? hematuria (kidney/urinary tract disease)
- Crystals ? possible kidney stones
- Casts ? kidney pathology (type-dependent)
- Cloudy urine ? infection or contamination

------------------------------------------------------------

Note:
- Results must always be correlated with symptoms
- Sample contamination can affect epithelial cells and bacteria count
- Reference ranges may vary by laboratory
- Dipstick tests are screening tools; microscopy confirms diagnosis
