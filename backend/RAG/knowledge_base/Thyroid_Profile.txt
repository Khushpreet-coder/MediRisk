Test: Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)

Description:
Thyroid function tests evaluate how well the thyroid gland is working. The thyroid regulates metabolism, energy production, and multiple body functions through hormone secretion. These tests help diagnose conditions such as hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, thyroiditis, Graves’ disease, and Hashimoto’s disease. Thyroid evaluation is done using blood tests that measure hormone and antibody levels.

Parameters:
TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
T3 (Triiodothyronine)
Free T3
T4 (Thyroxine)
Free T4
TPO Antibodies (Thyroid Peroxidase Antibodies)
TG (Thyroglobulin)
TGAb (Thyroglobulin Antibodies)
TSI (Thyroid Stimulating Immunoglobulin)

Normal Ranges (Adults - Approx):
TSH: 0.4–4.0 mIU/L
T3 (Total): 80–200 ng/dL
Free T3: 2.3–4.2 pg/mL
T4 (Total): 5.0–12.0 µg/dL
Free T4: 0.8–1.8 ng/dL
TPO Antibodies: <35 IU/mL
TGAb: <20 IU/mL
TSI: <1.3 index

Interpretation:
High TSH ? Hypothyroidism
Low TSH ? Hyperthyroidism
High T3/T4 ? Hyperthyroidism
Low T3/T4 ? Hypothyroidism
Positive TPO/TG antibodies ? Autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto’s or Graves’)
Abnormal TSI ? Graves’ disease

Note:
TSH is primary screening marker
Free T3/T4 are more accurate than total values
Antibodies indicate autoimmune disease
Ranges may vary by lab
Medications, stress, and timing can affect results
