Test: Blood Glucose Test (Fasting & Random)

Purpose:
Blood glucose tests measure the amount of glucose (sugar) in the blood. They are mainly used to screen, diagnose, and monitor diabetes (Type 1, Type 2, and gestational diabetes).

Types of Test:
- Fasting Blood Glucose (FBG): Measures glucose after 8–12 hours of fasting
- Random Blood Glucose (RBG): Measured at any time, regardless of meals
- Postprandial Blood Glucose (PPBG): Measured 2 hours after eating
- Capillary Test: Finger prick test using glucose meter (instant result)
- Venous Plasma Glucose: Lab-based blood draw (more accurate)

Parameters:
- Blood Glucose (Fasting)
- Blood Glucose (Random)
- Blood Glucose (Postprandial)
- Capillary Glucose Level
- Venous Plasma Glucose

Normal Ranges (Adults):

Fasting Blood Glucose:
70–99 mg/dL (Normal)
100–125 mg/dL (Prediabetes)
?126 mg/dL (Diabetes)

Random Blood Glucose:
<140 mg/dL (Normal)
?200 mg/dL (Possible diabetes)

Postprandial (2 hours after meal):
<140 mg/dL (Normal)
140–199 mg/dL (Prediabetes)
?200 mg/dL (Diabetes)

Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia):
<70 mg/dL

Interpretation:
- High fasting glucose ? Prediabetes or Diabetes
- High post-meal glucose ? Impaired glucose tolerance or Diabetes
- Low glucose ? Hypoglycemia (can occur due to medications, liver disease, fasting, or hormonal issues)
- Persistent high levels ? Insulin resistance or pancreatic dysfunction

Note:
- Fasting test requires 8–12 hours no food (water allowed)
- Stress, illness, and medications can affect results
- Always compare with lab reference ranges
- HbA1c is used for long-term glucose monitoring
