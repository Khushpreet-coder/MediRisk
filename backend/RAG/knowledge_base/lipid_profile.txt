Test: Lipid Profile (Lipid Panel / Cholesterol Panel / Lipid Test)

Purpose:
A lipid profile is a blood test used to measure fats (lipids) in the blood. It helps assess cardiovascular health and risk of diseases such as heart disease, heart attack, and stroke.

It is used to:
- Evaluate cholesterol levels
- Detect risk of cardiovascular disease
- Monitor response to lipid-lowering treatment
- Identify conditions like liver disease and pancreatitis

Parameters:
- Total Cholesterol
- Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL) Cholesterol
- High-Density Lipoprotein (HDL) Cholesterol
- Very Low-Density Lipoprotein (VLDL) Cholesterol
- Triglycerides

Parameter Details:
Total Cholesterol = overall cholesterol level (LDL + HDL + VLDL)

LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein):
- Known as �bad cholesterol�
- High levels increase risk of artery blockage and heart disease

HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein):
- Known as �good cholesterol�
- Helps remove LDL from blood vessels

VLDL (Very Low-Density Lipoprotein):
- Mainly carries triglycerides
- High levels may indicate poor fat metabolism

Triglycerides:
- Fat from food stored in blood
- High levels linked to heart disease and pancreatitis

Interpretation:
- High LDL ? increased risk of cardiovascular disease
- Low HDL ? higher risk of heart disease
- High triglycerides ? risk of heart disease and pancreatitis
- High total cholesterol ? overall lipid imbalance

Preparation:
- Fasting for 9�12 hours may be required (water allowed)
- Avoid food intake before test if fasting is advised by doctor

Note:
- Reference ranges may vary between laboratories
- Results should always be interpreted using lab-specific ranges
- Lipid levels are influenced by diet, lifestyle, genetics, and medications
