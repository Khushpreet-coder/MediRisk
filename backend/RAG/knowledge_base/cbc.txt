Test: Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Purpose:
CBC is used to evaluate overall health and detect conditions such as anemia, infection, and blood disorders.

Parameters:
- WBC (White Blood Cell count)
- RBC (Red Blood Cell count)
- Hemoglobin (Hb)
- Hematocrit (Hct)
- MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume)
- MCHC (Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration)
- RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width)
- Platelet Count
- MPV (Mean Platelet Volume)
- Neutrophils
- Lymphocytes
- Monocytes
- Eosinophils
- Basophils
- Immature Granulocytes
- NRBC (Nucleated Red Blood Cells)

Normal Ranges (Adults - Approx):

WBC: 4,000–10,000 cells/µL  
RBC:  
- Female: 4.0–5.4 million/µL  
- Male: 4.5–6.1 million/µL  

Hemoglobin (Hb):  
- Female: 11.5–15.5 g/dL  
- Male: 13–17 g/dL  

Hematocrit (Hct):  
- Female: 36%–48%  
- Male: 40%–55%  

MCV: 80–100 fL  
MCHC: 32–36 g/dL  
RDW: 12%–15%  
Platelet Count: 150,000–400,000 /µL  
MPV: 7.0–9.0 fL  

Neutrophils: 2,500–7,000 /µL  
Lymphocytes: 1,000–4,800 /µL  
Monocytes: 200–800 /µL  
Eosinophils: <500 /µL  
Basophils: <300 /µL  
Immature Granulocytes: <100 /µL  
NRBC: <10 /µL  

Interpretation:
- Low Hb ? anemia  
- High WBC ? infection or inflammation  
- Low WBC ? immune suppression  
- High Platelets ? inflammation or infection  
- Low Platelets ? bleeding risk  
- Abnormal differential counts ? possible immune or blood disorders  

Note:
- Reference ranges may vary by lab  
- Always prioritize report-provided ranges over general values  
- Units may differ (cells/µL, k/µL, million/µL)
