TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT / RFT)
PURPOSE: Evaluates how well the kidneys filter waste and maintain fluid and electrolyte balance. Used to detect kidney disease, monitor diabetes or hypertension, and assess kidney damage.

---

PARAMETER: Serum Creatinine
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Creatinine is a waste product of muscle metabolism filtered out by the kidneys. Elevated serum creatinine indicates reduced kidney filtering ability.
Normal Range (Female): 0.6–1.1 mg/dL
Normal Range (Male): 0.7–1.3 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: Low creatinine may be seen in low muscle mass, malnutrition, or pregnancy.
High Interpretation: High creatinine suggests kidney dysfunction, acute kidney injury, dehydration, or chronic kidney disease.

---

PARAMETER: Creatinine Clearance
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mL/min
Meaning: Measures how efficiently the kidneys clear creatinine from the blood per minute. Provides an estimate of kidney filtering rate.
Normal Range (Female): 88–128 mL/min
Normal Range (Male): 97–137 mL/min
Low Interpretation: Low creatinine clearance indicates impaired kidney filtering and possible chronic kidney disease.
High Interpretation: High values may reflect high muscle turnover or high protein diet; generally not concerning.
Note: Measured using 24-hour urine collection and serum creatinine together.

---

PARAMETER: GFR / eGFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate)
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mL/min/1.73 m²
Meaning: GFR is the most important indicator of overall kidney health. It estimates how much blood the kidneys filter per minute per body surface area.
Normal Range: > 90 mL/min/1.73 m²
Average Healthy Adult: 90–140 mL/min/1.73 m²
Mildly Reduced: 60–89 mL/min/1.73 m²
Moderately Reduced: 30–59 mL/min/1.73 m²
Severely Reduced: 15–29 mL/min/1.73 m²
Kidney Failure: < 15 mL/min/1.73 m²
Low Interpretation: Low GFR indicates reduced kidney filtration. Values below 60 for 3+ months indicate chronic kidney disease (CKD). Below 15 may require dialysis.
High Interpretation: High GFR is generally not a concern. May indicate hyperfiltration in early diabetes.

---

PARAMETER: Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: BUN measures nitrogen waste in the blood from protein breakdown. Kidneys filter urea out; elevated levels suggest impaired kidney function.
Normal Range: 7–20 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: Low BUN may indicate liver disease, malnutrition, or overhydration.
High Interpretation: High BUN suggests kidney impairment, dehydration, high protein diet, or gastrointestinal bleeding.
Note: BUN is more useful when interpreted alongside creatinine (BUN:Creatinine ratio). Normal ratio is 10:1 to 20:1.

---

PARAMETER: Urine Albumin
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mg/dL
Meaning: Albumin is a protein that healthy kidneys prevent from leaking into urine. Its presence indicates kidney damage to the filtration barrier.
Normal Range: 0–8 mg/dL
Low Interpretation: Normal finding.
High Interpretation: Elevated urine albumin indicates proteinuria — a sign of kidney damage, diabetes-related nephropathy, or hypertensive kidney disease.

---

PARAMETER: Urine Microalbumin
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mg/day
Meaning: Detects small (micro) amounts of albumin in urine not detectable on standard urinalysis. Early marker of kidney damage.
Normal Range: < 30 mg/day
Early Kidney Disease: 30–300 mg/day (microalbuminuria)
Advanced Kidney Disease: > 300 mg/day (macroalbuminuria)
Low Interpretation: Normal finding — no kidney damage detected.
High Interpretation: 30–300 mg/day = early kidney damage (microalbuminuria). > 300 mg/day = advanced kidney damage requiring urgent management.

---

PARAMETER: Albumin to Creatinine Ratio (ACR)
TEST: Kidney Function Tests (KFT)
Unit: mg/g
Meaning: ACR compares the amount of albumin to creatinine in a spot urine sample. It is a convenient indicator of protein leakage from the kidneys.
Normal Range: < 30 mg/g
Moderately Increased: 30–300 mg/g
Severely Increased: > 300 mg/g
Low Interpretation: Normal kidney filtration.
High Interpretation: Elevated ACR indicates protein leakage due to kidney disease. Used for staging chronic kidney disease alongside GFR.
