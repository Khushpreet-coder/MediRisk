Test: Kidney Function Tests (KFT / RFT)

Purpose:
Kidney function tests evaluate how well the kidneys are filtering waste and maintaining fluid and electrolyte balance. They help detect kidney disease, monitor chronic conditions like diabetes or hypertension, and assess kidney damage or dysfunction.
Parameters:
Serum Creatinine
Creatinine Clearance
Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR / eGFR)
Urine Albumin
Urine Microalbumin
Albumin to Creatinine Ratio (ACR)
Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
Normal Ranges (Adults - Approx):
Serum Creatinine:
Female: 0.6–1.1 mg/dL
Male: 0.7–1.3 mg/dL
Creatinine Clearance:
Female: 88–128 mL/min
Male: 97–137 mL/min
Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR):
Normal: > 90 mL/min/1.73 m²
Average healthy adult: ~90–140 mL/min/1.73 m²
Kidney failure: < 15 mL/min/1.73 m²
Urine Albumin:
Normal: 0–8 mg/dL
Urine Microalbumin:
Normal: < 30 mg/day
Early kidney disease: 30–300 mg/day
Advanced kidney disease: > 300 mg/day
Albumin to Creatinine Ratio (ACR):
Normal: < 30 mg/g
Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN):
Normal: 7–20 mg/dL
Interpretation:
High creatinine ? possible kidney dysfunction
Low GFR ? reduced kidney filtering ability
High BUN ? kidney impairment or dehydration or high protein breakdown
High albumin / microalbumin ? early kidney damage
High ACR ? protein leakage in urine (kidney disease indicator)
Note:
Reference ranges may vary by lab
Always prioritize report-provided values
Kidney function depends on age, hydration, muscle mass, and medical conditions
GFR is the most important overall kidney health indicator
