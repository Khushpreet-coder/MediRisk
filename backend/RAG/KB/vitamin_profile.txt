Test: Vitamin Profile (Common Vitamin Tests)

Purpose:
Vitamin profile evaluates essential vitamin levels in blood to detect deficiencies or excess affecting energy metabolism, nerves, immunity, blood formation, and bone health.

Parameters:
- Vitamin D (25-Hydroxy Vitamin D)
- Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)
- Folate (Vitamin B9)
- Vitamin A (Retinol)
- Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

------------------------------------------------------------

Vitamin D (25-Hydroxy Vitamin D)

Normal Ranges:
- < 20 ng/mL ? Deficiency
- 20–30 ng/mL ? Insufficiency
- 30–100 ng/mL ? Normal
- > 100 ng/mL ? Toxicity risk

Interpretation:
- Low ? bone weakness, fatigue, low immunity
- High ? calcium imbalance, toxicity risk

------------------------------------------------------------

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Normal Ranges:
- < 200 pg/mL ? Deficiency
- 200–900 pg/mL ? Normal
- > 900 pg/mL ? Possible excess

Interpretation:
- Low ? anemia, neuropathy, fatigue, memory issues
- High ? supplementation or liver/metabolic issues

------------------------------------------------------------

Folate (Vitamin B9)

Normal Ranges:
- < 2 ng/mL ? Deficiency
- 2–20 ng/mL ? Normal
- > 20 ng/mL ? High

Interpretation:
- Low ? megaloblastic anemia, weakness, poor DNA synthesis
- High ? supplementation, may mask B12 deficiency

------------------------------------------------------------

Vitamin A (Retinol)

Normal Ranges:
- 20–60 µg/dL ? Normal
- < 20 µg/dL ? Deficiency
- > 60 µg/dL ? Toxicity risk

Interpretation:
- Low ? night blindness, dry skin, immune weakness
- High ? liver toxicity, headache, nausea

------------------------------------------------------------

Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

Normal Ranges:
- 5–20 mg/L ? Normal
- < 5 mg/L ? Deficiency
- > 20 mg/L ? Excess intake

Interpretation:
- Low ? nerve damage, muscle weakness
- High ? bleeding risk (affects clotting balance)

------------------------------------------------------------

Note:
- Reference ranges may vary across laboratories
- Vitamins are often tested individually rather than as a single panel
- Deficiencies often overlap with anemia, fatigue, and neurological symptoms
