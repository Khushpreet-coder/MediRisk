TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
PURPOSE: Blood tests used to evaluate liver health. Detect liver disease, inflammation, damage, bile duct function, and response to medications or toxins.

---

PARAMETER: ALT (Alanine Transaminase / SGPT)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: IU/L
Meaning: ALT is an enzyme found primarily in liver cells. When liver cells are damaged, ALT is released into the blood. It is the most specific marker of liver cell injury.
Normal Range: 0–45 IU/L
Low Interpretation: Normal or not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: Elevated ALT indicates liver cell damage. Causes include hepatitis (viral, alcoholic, autoimmune), fatty liver disease, medication toxicity, or cirrhosis. Very high levels (> 10x normal) suggest acute hepatitis.

---

PARAMETER: AST (Aspartate Transaminase / SGOT)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: IU/L
Meaning: AST is found in liver, heart, and muscle cells. Less specific than ALT for liver disease but elevated in liver injury and muscle damage.
Normal Range: 0–35 IU/L
Low Interpretation: Normal or not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: Elevated AST suggests liver injury or muscle disease. AST:ALT ratio > 2:1 often indicates alcoholic liver disease. Isolated high AST may also point to cardiac or skeletal muscle damage.

---

PARAMETER: ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: U/L
Meaning: ALP is produced in the liver (bile ducts), bone, kidneys, and intestines. Elevation is associated with bile duct obstruction or liver/bone disorders.
Normal Range: 30–120 U/L
Low Interpretation: Low ALP may indicate hypothyroidism, anemia, or malnutrition.
High Interpretation: High ALP suggests bile duct obstruction (cholestasis), primary biliary cholangitis, liver infiltration (cancer/abscess), or bone disorders (Paget's disease, bone metastases).

---

PARAMETER: GGT (Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: IU/L
Meaning: GGT is a liver enzyme sensitive to bile duct disease and alcohol-related liver damage. Used alongside ALP to differentiate liver from bone causes.
Normal Range: 0–30 IU/L
Low Interpretation: Normal finding.
High Interpretation: Elevated GGT strongly suggests bile duct disease, alcohol abuse, or fatty liver. If both ALP and GGT are high, liver/bile duct origin is confirmed.

---

PARAMETER: Bilirubin (Total)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: µmol/L or mg/dL
Meaning: Bilirubin is a yellow waste product from red blood cell breakdown processed by the liver. High levels cause jaundice (yellow skin/eyes).
Normal Range: 2–17 µmol/L (or 0.1–1.2 mg/dL)
Low Interpretation: Not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: High total bilirubin indicates liver dysfunction, bile duct obstruction, or excessive red blood cell breakdown (hemolysis). Causes jaundice. Direct bilirubin elevation points to liver/bile duct disease; indirect elevation suggests hemolysis.

---

PARAMETER: Albumin
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: g/dL
Meaning: Albumin is the primary protein made by the liver. It reflects long-term liver function and nutritional status. Low levels indicate chronic liver impairment.
Normal Range: 3.5–5.0 g/dL
Low Interpretation: Low albumin suggests chronic liver disease (cirrhosis), malnutrition, nephrotic syndrome, or severe infection. It is a marker of reduced liver synthetic function.
High Interpretation: High albumin is uncommon and may indicate dehydration.

---

PARAMETER: Total Protein
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: g/dL
Meaning: Total protein measures albumin plus globulin in blood. Reflects liver function, immune activity, and nutritional status.
Normal Range: 6.3–7.9 g/dL
Low Interpretation: Low total protein may indicate liver dysfunction, malnutrition, or protein-losing kidney or gut disease.
High Interpretation: High total protein may indicate dehydration, chronic infection, or conditions that increase immunoglobulins (e.g., multiple myeloma).

---

PARAMETER: LDH (Lactate Dehydrogenase)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: U/L
Meaning: LDH is an enzyme found in many tissues. Elevated levels indicate tissue damage or cell breakdown, including in the liver.
Normal Range: 122–222 U/L
Low Interpretation: Not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: High LDH suggests liver damage, hemolytic anemia, myocardial infarction, muscle injury, or cancer. Not specific to the liver — must be interpreted with other markers.

---

PARAMETER: Prothrombin Time (PT)
TEST: Liver Function Tests (LFT)
Unit: seconds
Meaning: PT measures the time it takes blood to clot. The liver produces clotting factors; a prolonged PT indicates reduced liver synthetic function.
Normal Range: 9.4–12.5 seconds
Low Interpretation: Shorter PT is not typically significant.
High Interpretation: Prolonged PT indicates the liver is not producing enough clotting factors. This occurs in acute liver failure, cirrhosis, or vitamin K deficiency. Critical in assessing severity of liver disease.
