TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
PURPOSE: Evaluates overall health and detects conditions such as anemia, infection, and blood disorders.

---

PARAMETER: WBC (White Blood Cell Count)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: cells/µL
Meaning: WBC (white blood cells) are immune cells that fight infection and inflammation.
Normal Range: 4,000–10,000 cells/µL
Low Interpretation: Low WBC (leukopenia) may indicate immune suppression, bone marrow disorder, or viral infection.
High Interpretation: High WBC (leukocytosis) may indicate bacterial infection, inflammation, stress, or leukemia.

---

PARAMETER: RBC (Red Blood Cell Count)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: million/µL
Meaning: RBCs carry oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body.
Normal Range (Female): 4.0–5.4 million/µL
Normal Range (Male): 4.5–6.1 million/µL
Low Interpretation: Low RBC may indicate anemia, blood loss, or nutritional deficiency.
High Interpretation: High RBC may indicate dehydration, lung disease, or polycythemia.

---

PARAMETER: Hemoglobin (Hb)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: g/dL
Meaning: Hemoglobin is the protein in RBCs that carries oxygen. It is the primary indicator of anemia.
Normal Range (Female): 11.5–15.5 g/dL
Normal Range (Male): 13.0–17.0 g/dL
Low Interpretation: Low hemoglobin indicates anemia. Causes include iron deficiency, blood loss, B12/folate deficiency, or chronic disease.
High Interpretation: High hemoglobin may indicate dehydration, polycythemia vera, or high-altitude adaptation.

---

PARAMETER: Hematocrit (Hct)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: %
Meaning: Hematocrit measures the percentage of blood volume made up of red blood cells.
Normal Range (Female): 36%–48%
Normal Range (Male): 40%–55%
Low Interpretation: Low hematocrit suggests anemia or overhydration.
High Interpretation: High hematocrit may indicate dehydration or polycythemia.

---

PARAMETER: MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: fL
Meaning: MCV measures the average size of red blood cells. It helps classify the type of anemia.
Normal Range: 80–100 fL
Low Interpretation: Low MCV (microcytic anemia) — commonly caused by iron deficiency or thalassemia.
High Interpretation: High MCV (macrocytic anemia) — commonly caused by B12 or folate deficiency, or liver disease.

---

PARAMETER: MCHC (Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: g/dL
Meaning: MCHC measures the concentration of hemoglobin within red blood cells.
Normal Range: 32–36 g/dL
Low Interpretation: Low MCHC (hypochromia) indicates iron deficiency anemia or thalassemia.
High Interpretation: High MCHC may indicate hereditary spherocytosis or severe dehydration.

---

PARAMETER: RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: %
Meaning: RDW measures variation in red blood cell size. Higher values indicate more size variation (anisocytosis).
Normal Range: 12%–15%
Low Interpretation: Low RDW is generally not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: High RDW suggests mixed anemia types (e.g., combined iron and B12 deficiency), or early nutritional deficiency.

---

PARAMETER: Platelet Count
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Platelets help stop bleeding by forming blood clots at injury sites.
Normal Range: 150,000–400,000 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low platelets (thrombocytopenia) indicate increased bleeding risk. Causes include viral infection, autoimmune disease, or bone marrow suppression.
High Interpretation: High platelets (thrombocytosis) may indicate inflammation, infection, iron deficiency, or myeloproliferative disorder.

---

PARAMETER: MPV (Mean Platelet Volume)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: fL
Meaning: MPV measures the average size of platelets. Larger platelets are generally more active.
Normal Range: 7.0–9.0 fL
Low Interpretation: Low MPV may indicate bone marrow suppression or aplastic anemia.
High Interpretation: High MPV may indicate platelet destruction, cardiovascular disease risk, or immune thrombocytopenia.

---

PARAMETER: Neutrophils
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Neutrophils are the most common WBC type. They are the first responders to bacterial infection.
Normal Range: 2,500–7,000 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low neutrophils (neutropenia) increase susceptibility to bacterial infections.
High Interpretation: High neutrophils (neutrophilia) indicate bacterial infection, stress, inflammation, or steroid use.

---

PARAMETER: Lymphocytes
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Lymphocytes are immune cells involved in viral defense and antibody production (B cells and T cells).
Normal Range: 1,000–4,800 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low lymphocytes may indicate viral infection (e.g., HIV), immunodeficiency, or steroid use.
High Interpretation: High lymphocytes (lymphocytosis) suggest viral infection (e.g., EBV, CMV) or lymphocytic leukemia.

---

PARAMETER: Monocytes
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Monocytes are large WBCs that fight infection and clean up dead cells. They become macrophages in tissues.
Normal Range: 200–800 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low monocytes are rarely significant but may occur with bone marrow disorders.
High Interpretation: High monocytes (monocytosis) may indicate chronic infection, autoimmune disease, or certain cancers.

---

PARAMETER: Eosinophils
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Eosinophils respond to allergic reactions and parasitic infections.
Normal Range: < 500 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low eosinophils are not typically clinically significant.
High Interpretation: High eosinophils (eosinophilia) suggest allergic conditions (asthma, eczema), parasitic infection, or drug reaction.

---

PARAMETER: Basophils
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Basophils are the least common WBC. They release histamine during allergic responses.
Normal Range: < 300 /µL
Low Interpretation: Low basophils are rarely significant.
High Interpretation: High basophils (basophilia) may indicate allergic reaction or chronic myeloid leukemia.

---

PARAMETER: Immature Granulocytes
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: Immature granulocytes (bands/metamyelocytes) are young WBCs released from bone marrow during severe infection or stress.
Normal Range: < 100 /µL
Low Interpretation: Not clinically significant.
High Interpretation: Elevated levels indicate severe infection, sepsis, or bone marrow stimulation.

---

PARAMETER: NRBC (Nucleated Red Blood Cells)
TEST: Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Unit: /µL
Meaning: NRBCs are immature red blood cells. Their presence in peripheral blood is abnormal in adults.
Normal Range: < 10 /µL
Low Interpretation: Not applicable.
High Interpretation: High NRBC in adults may indicate severe anemia, bone marrow disease, hemolysis, or hypoxia.
